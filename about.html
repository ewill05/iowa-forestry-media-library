About the Library

The Iowa Forestry Media Library (IFML) is an open-access initiative dedicated to digitizing and preserving high-resolution botanical imagery for forestry education and research.

Our Mission

Forestry students and professionals often rely on field guides with small, low-resolution printed images. The IFML seeks to solve this by providing 4K+ resolution scans of dendrology specimens, allowing users to zoom in on critical identification featuresâ€”from the texture of a winter bud to the venation of a leaf.

Specimens in this library are sourced from verified field collections within the Midwest region. We utilize high-fidelity flatbed scanning techniques to capture botanical details often lost in traditional photography.

Technical Standards

Resolution: 1200 DPI minimum (50+ megapixels per specimen)

File Format: Uncompressed 48-bit TIFF for archival; PNG for web preview

Color Management: Adobe RGB embedded profiles for scientific accuracy

Scale Verification: Documented pixels-per-millimeter for morphometric analysis

Storage: Permanent DOI assignment via Zenodo repository

Open Access Commitment

We believe that educational resources should be free. All data in this library is hosted publicly on Zenodo and GitHub, ensuring it remains accessible to students, researchers, and the public indefinitely.

If you use these images in research or educational materials, please cite the collection as follows:

Iowa Forestry Media Library. (2026). Digital Dendrology Archive. Retrieved from https://iowaforestrymedialibrary.org
