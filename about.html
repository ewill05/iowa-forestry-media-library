Digital Dendrology Methodology

The Iowa Forestry Media Library (IFML) is an open-access initiative dedicated to digitizing and preserving high-resolution botanical imagery for forestry education and research.

Standardized Preservation

Optical Standards

All specimens are captured at a resolution between 800 - 1200 DPI using calibrated scanning techniques to capture botanical details.

Color Integrity

We utilize lossless 24-bit color (and 48-bit for wood cookies) to preserve natural color and turgidity[cite: 2].

Collection Protocol

  • Freshness: Specimens are collected and scanned immediately to preserve natural botanical features[cite: 2].
  • Completeness: Every record includes foliage (leaves) and twigs (winter buds/scars) to support identification.
  • Persistence: Every dataset is assigned a permanent DOI via Zenodo for reliable citation[cite: 7].
